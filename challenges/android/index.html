Android mascot history

You probably know that Google's Android operating system is one of the most widely used mobile devices worldwide. But perhaps you didn't know that its friendly mascot had a name and an alluring story? Well, follow this article to learn a lot about this little robot.

First version

The first attempt at creating a mascot came in 2007 from a developer named Dan Morrill. He says he opened Inkscape (free software for vectorizing images) and created his own robot. The goal was to personify the system for his team; there was no request from the company to create a mascot.

This first bizarre version was even named after its creator: they would be the Dandroids.

A new mascot arises

The idea of having a mascot matured, and the task was passed on to a professional in the field. Russian illustrator Irina Blok, also a Google employee, was tasked with representing the little robot in a more appealing way.

Irina's main idea was to represent everything graphically with fewer lines and in a flatter way. The drawing should also be easily identifiable to anyone who sees it. Thus, Bugdroid, Android's new mascot, was born.

The main inspiration for the new Bugdroid's design came from those little figures that illustrate bathroom doors to indicate the type of door. Legend has it that the artist was creating at her desk in the Google office and looked at the restrooms, and the idea was immediately recognizable: simple, clean, and to the point.

Want to learn more?

Another curious thing about Android is that each version has always been named after a sweet, in alphabetical order from version 1.5 to 9.0.

  • 1.5 - Cupcake
  • 1.6 - Donut
  • 2.0 - Eclair
  • 2.2 - Froyo
  • 2.3 - Gingerbread
  • 3.0 - Honeycomb
  • 4.0 - Ice Cream Sandwich
  • 4.1 - Jelly Bean
  • 4.4 - KitKat
  • 5.0 - Lollipop
  • 6.0 - Marshmallow
  • 7.0 - Nougat
  • 8.0 - Oreo
  • 9.0 - Pie

Unfortunately, Android Q never happened, as Google decided to end this fun practice and began using numbering, which gave rise to Android 10.
